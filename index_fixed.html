About ENEMS

An innovation lab, not a casual club

Unlike traditional student organizations, ENEMS functions as a working innovation lab where every project moves beyond ideation toward measurable implementation impact.

ENEMS operates at the intersection of device design, systems analysis, and clinical reasoning. We build solutions that work in real EMS environments — not just in theory.

Members engage in structured problem identification, evidence review, prototyping, validation, and publication. Each initiative requires ownership, documentation, and structured progression from problem definition to validated outcome.

ENEMS sits between a medical device startup, a clinical research lab, and a systems engineering unit — all within a student-led framework.

Engineering Division

Devices Team

The Devices Team builds medical solutions from the ground up. Projects begin with a defined operational gap in prehospital care and progress through structured development stages.

Members conduct structured literature reviews, evaluate current standards, generate engineering concepts, and develop iterative prototypes. Emphasis is placed on real-world constraints — workflow integration, field durability, usability, and scalability.

The goal is not concept design, but functional, testable systems ready for deployment pathways.

Prototyping Device Design Literature Review Validation Deployment Pathways Field Durability

What Members Do

Each project follows a structured progression from problem identification to validated outcome.

Project Lifecycle

  • Define operational gap in prehospital care
  • Conduct structured literature review
  • Evaluate current standards and solutions
  • Generate and assess engineering concepts
  • Develop iterative prototypes
  • Validate against real-world constraints
  • Document and publish findings
Research Division

Systems Team

The Systems Team focuses on identifying inefficiencies and structural gaps within EMS environments — spanning workflow optimization, clinical protocol evaluation, and meta-analysis.

The team produces structured analyses, performance evaluations, and publishable findings that strengthen evidence-based implementation and support broader institutional or policy-level improvements.

Where the Devices Team builds things, the Systems Team asks: how can existing systems work better?

Systems Analysis Meta-Analysis Protocol Review Workflow Optimization Publication Policy Impact

Research Focus Areas

Systems Team projects are grounded in rigorous evidence review and structured methodology. Every analysis is built toward a publishable, implementable outcome.

Core Work Areas

  • Workflow optimization in prehospital settings
  • Clinical protocol evaluation and comparison
  • Systems-level inefficiency identification
  • Structured meta-analysis and literature synthesis
  • Performance evaluation and benchmarking
  • Institutional and policy-level recommendations
