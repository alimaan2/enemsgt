About ENEMS

An innovation lab, not a casual club

Unlike traditional student organizations, ENEMS functions as a working innovation lab where every project moves beyond ideation toward measurable implementation impact.

ENEMS operates at the intersection of device design, systems analysis, and clinical reasoning. We build solutions that work in real EMS environments — not just in theory.

Members engage in structured problem identification, evidence review, prototyping, validation, and publication. Each initiative requires ownership, documentation, and structured progression from problem definition to validated outcome.

ENEMS sits between a medical device startup, a clinical research lab, and a systems engineering unit — all within a student-led framework.

How We Work

Our Core Principles

  • Research-informed decision making
  • Iterative development and prototyping
  • Systems-level thinking
  • Cross-disciplinary collaboration
  • Measurable validation
  • Publication and knowledge dissemination

Project Methodology

ENEMS Project Plan

All Devices Team projects follow this stepwise order. No phase is skipped — each unlocks the next.

Phase 1
Define the Problem Clearly
01
Problem Statement — must be finalized before anything else
  • What exactly are we solving, and why does it need to be solved?
  • Who is the target audience, and how urgent is this issue?
  • How feasible is implementation in a real EMS environment?
Phase 2
Market & Research Review
02
Current Solutions Analysis
  • Identify existing solutions and build a Pros & Cons list
  • Identify gaps in the field and determine what is lacking
  • Analyze costs of current solutions
03
Literature Search & Evidence Review
  • Find peer-reviewed studies; include adjacent and overlapping fields
  • Collect testimonials and real-world data from the field
  • Write structured summaries — identify methods and what we can adapt
  • All summaries compiled into one organized reference document
Phase 3
Stakeholder Validation
04
Interview EMS & Medical Faculty
  • Ask about current workflow and identify pain points
  • Validate whether our proposed solution addresses real needs
  • Determine what is currently working vs. failing
Phase 4
Team & Infrastructure Planning
05
Map Group Skills & Allocation
  • Assess current team strengths and determine how many people are needed
  • Assign roles strategically based on project requirements
06
Recruit Medical Faculty Advisors
  • Secure oversight for the design phase and gain clinical feedback during development
  • Strengthen credibility and implementation pathway
Phase 5
Development Preparation
07
Begin Structured Design Planning — only after research + interviews are complete
  • Refine design goals and define hard constraints
  • Outline prototype roadmap and set measurable performance benchmarks
Throughout
Supporting Materials
Lit ReviewAnnotated summaries of all sources
Interview NotesSynthesis of EMS & faculty sessions
Gap AnalysisStructured document of unmet needs
Market SheetCost & comparison of current solutions
Research Division

Systems Team

The Systems Team focuses on identifying inefficiencies and structural gaps within EMS environments — spanning workflow optimization, clinical protocol evaluation, and meta-analysis.

The team produces structured analyses, performance evaluations, and publishable findings that strengthen evidence-based implementation and support broader institutional or policy-level improvements.

Where the Devices Team builds things, the Systems Team asks: how can existing systems work better?

Systems Analysis Meta-Analysis Protocol Review Workflow Optimization Publication Policy Impact

Research Focus Areas

Systems Team projects are grounded in rigorous evidence review and structured methodology. Every analysis is built toward a publishable, implementable outcome.

Core Work Areas

  • Workflow optimization in prehospital settings
  • Clinical protocol evaluation and comparison
  • Systems-level inefficiency identification
  • Structured meta-analysis and literature synthesis
  • Performance evaluation and benchmarking
  • Institutional and policy-level recommendations
